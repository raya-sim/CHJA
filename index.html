rules and membership information

The CHJA is the governing body for the Hunter and Showjumping disciplines in Counter Canter. The CHJA hosts and sponsors shows yearround for both disciplines. Membership includes:

  • Eligibility for invitation-only shows and year-end awards.
  • Tracking of special horse and rider accomplishments.
  • The opportunity to compete in special classes and series.
Membership
Membership is open to all Counter Canter players. Players must be members to enter horses in official CHJA shows and to be eligible for year-end awards. See the association page for more information on dues.
Shows and awards
The CHJA hosts shows throughout the year. Horses accrue points for year-end awards by entering all hunter and showjumping shows. However, some special classes are only offered at CHJA shows and tracked separately.
Autoentry
Members can opt into autoentry by paying the autoentry dues on the website. Unlike other associations, AUTOENTRIES SHOULD BE SUBMITTED VIA GOOGLE SPREADSHEET. Contact an association owner (Diana, Raya, or Ryan) for access to the Google Sheet. Enter shows via the forum or via the autoentry spreadsheet. Note that entries require a rider. Classes and divisions have their own eligibility requirements which apply to both horses and riders. See classlists below for requirements for each division.
Riders
All CHJA entries require a rider! This is unique in CC, and allows tracking points and accomplishments by rider of horse. Enter shows via the forum or via the autoentry spreadsheet. Newly trained hunter horses can begin at any starred (★) level. Unless otherwise noted, hunter divisions include one under saddle class, one over fences, and one handy hunter over fences. Note that hunters typically stay in one division for an entire season.

Green Hunter Divisions

Green hunter divisions are for horses early in their career and eligiblity is limited by the amount of time a horse has been competing over fences at a certain height. Professional riders are strongly encouraged.

Baby Green Hunter ★

Fences 2'6". Open to horses 4 years and older who have never shown over 2'9" in their first season of showing over fences. Horses may only show in this division for one season.

Green Hunter 3ft ★

Open to horses 4 years and older who have never competed at or above 3'3". Horses may only show in this division for one season.

Green Hunter 3ft3 ★

Open to horses 4 years and older who have never competed at or above 3'6". Horses may only show in this division for one season.

Green Hunter 3ft6 ★

Open to horses 5 years and older in their first year competing at 3'6", who have never competed above 3'9". Horses may only show in this division for one season.

Green Hunter 3ft9

Open to horses 5 years and older in their second year competing at or above 3'6". Horses may only show in this division for one season.

Performance Hunter Divisions

Horses may compete in performance hunter divisions indefinitely. Professional riders strongly encouraged.

Performance Hunter 3ft6

Open to horses 5 years and older in their second year or more of competing at or above 3'6".

High Performance Hunter

Open to horses 6 years and older in their second year or more of competing at or above 3'6".

Pony Hunter Divisions

Pony divisions are open to ponies 14.2hh and smaller ridden by junior riders (17 and younger). Small ponies are 12.2hh and below and must ridden by riders 12 years and younger. Medium ponies are 12.3-13.2hh and must be ridden by riders 14 and younger. Large ponies are 13.2-14.2hh and must be ridden by riders 17 and younger.

Green Pony Hunter ★

Open to ponies 4 years and older in their first year of showing over fences. Must be ridden by a junior rider. Fences 2'0" for smalls, 2'3" for mediums, 2'9" for larges.

Regular Pony Hunter

Open to ponies 5 years and older in their second year or more of showing over fences. Must be ridden by a junior rider. Fences 2'3" for smalls, 2'6" for mediums, 3'0" for larges.

Rider-restricted Divisions

Rider-restricted divisions are for horses ridden by juniors (17 years and younger) or amateurs (adult riders who do not receive remuneration for activities related to riding, training, or instruction of horses or riders). Horses may compete in rider-restricted divisions indefinitely.

Children's/Adult Amateur Hunter

Open to horses ridden by junior or adult amateur riders who have never competed at or above 3'6". Fences 3'0"

Junior Hunter

Open to horses ridden by junior riders. Fences 3'6"

Amateur Owner Hunter

Open to horses ridden by amateur adult riders who own the horse or are a member of the owners family. Fences 3'6"

Newly trained jumpers must start at one of the starred (★) levels and must receive 5 or more top-3 placings (1st, 2nd, or 3rd) before advancing to the next level. Unless otherwise noted, jumper divisions include three rounds.

Special classes are held every other week (B weeks in the schedule). Horses may enter one special division or up to three special classes for which they are eligible.

Special Hunter Divisions

Special Hunter Divisions consist of two or more classes. Horses entered into a special division may not enter any special classes.

Young Hunter Showcase

Open to horses 4 and 5 years old competing in Green Hunter divisions. Fences 2’6”. Two rounds: one Under Saddle and one Over Fences. Top young hunters at the end of the season will be invited for a final showcase and year-end awards.

Special Hunter Classes

Special Hunter Classes consist of one class, or round, each. Horses may enter up to three special classes, but may not cross-enter into any special divisions.

Pony Hunter Derby

Open to ponies (14.2hh and under) competing in Green Pony Hunters or Regular Pony Hunters and ridden by junior riders. Fences at ponies’ respective division heights. One round. Top ponies at the end of the season will be invited for a final invitational derby and rider results tracked for year-end awards.

National Hunter Derby

Open to horses only. Fences 3'0"-3’5”. One round. May not cross into the International Hunter Derby. Top horses at the end of the season will be invited for a final invitational derby and rider results tracked for year-end awards.

International Hunter Derby

Open to horses only. Fences 3'9" to 4'6". One round. May not cross into the National Hunter Derby. Top horses at the end of the season will be invited for a final invitational derby and rider results tracked for year-end awards.

CHJA Pony Medal

Open to junior riders under 18 mounted on ponies. Smalls (12.2hh and under) must be ridden by a rider under 13. Fences 2'3" for smalls, 2'6" for mediums, 3'0" for larges. Suitability of a pony to rider will be emphasized. Riders who win a CHJA Pony Medal class throughout the season will be invited to compete in Pony Medal Finals.

CHJA Hunt Seat Equitation Medal

Open to junior riders under 18 mounted on horses. Fences 3'6". One round. Riders may not compete in the CHJA Pony Medal. Riders who win a CHJA Hunt Seat Medal class throughout the season will be invited to compete in Medal Finals.

Special Jumper Divisions

Special Jumper Divisions consist of two or more classes. Horses entered into a special division may not enter any special classes.

Pony Jumper

Open to ponies (14.2hh and below) 5 years and older ridden by junior riders. Fences 1.10M. Three rounds. Pony Jumper results will be tracked like open jumper divisions for year-end awards.

Children's/Adult Amateur Jumper

Open to horses or ponies 4 years old or older, to be ridden by junior or amateur owner riders only. Fences 1.05M. Three rounds. Riders may not cross enter into Junior/Amateur Owner Jumpers. Children's/Adult Amateur Jumper results will be tracked like open jumper divisions for year-end awards.

Junior/Amateur Owner Jumper

Open to horses 6 years old or older eligible to compete at or above 1.30M, to be ridden by junior or amateur owner or an amateur member of the owner’s family only. Fences 1.30M. Three rounds. Riders may not cross enter into Children’s or Pony Jumper or Hunter classes. Junior/Amateur Owner Jumper results will be tracked like open jumper divisions for year-end awards.

Young Jumper

Open to horses 4, 5, and 6 years old. Horses must be eligible to compete at the respective height for their age. Fences 1.05M for 4-year-olds, 1.20M for 5-year-olds, 1.30M for 6-year-olds. Three rounds. Young Jumper results will be tracked like open jumper divisions for year-end awards.

International Jumper

Open to horses 8 years or older eligible to compete at or above 1.50M. Professional riders strongly encouraged. To consist of three rounds: a Welcome Prix, a Speed Classic, and a Grand Prix. Top horses at the end of the season will be invited for a final championship series and rider results tracked for year-end awards.

Special Jumper Classes

Special Jumper Classes consist of one class, or round, each. Horses may enter up to three special classes, but may not cross-enter into any special divisions.

CHJA Show Jumping Talent Search

Open to junior and young riders aged 21 and under. Non-professionals only. Fences 3'9" to 4'0". One round. Riders who win a CHJA Show Jumping Talent Search class throughout the season will be invited to compete in Medal Finals.
